Why Housekeeping Careers Remain Strong

  • Stable demand: Hotels, hospitals, offices, and households consistently need cleaning staff.
  • Flexible schedules: Many employers offer part-time, evening, or weekend shifts.
  • Entry-friendly: Little to no formal education required, with on-the-job training common.
  • Physical, active work: Suitable for individuals who enjoy movement and routine tasks.
  • Pathways to advancement: Supervisory roles and facility management often start with housekeeping.

Expected Pay Ranges

Rates vary by region, employer, and experience. Here’s what most US housekeepers can expect:

  • Entry-level hospitality roles – $14–$18/hour
  • Residential housekeeping – $16–$22/hour
  • Hospital & medical facilities – $17–$24/hour
  • Supervisory or senior staff – $20–$30/hour

Full-time positions often include benefits such as paid time off, retirement plans, or healthcare—especially in larger organizations.

Skills & Equipment Essentials

Core Competencies

  • Speed and accuracy in routine cleaning
  • Safe handling of chemicals and supplies
  • Strong work ethic and punctuality
  • Communication with supervisors and residents

Helpful Equipment

  • Quality vacuum cleaner and dusting tools
  • Microfiber cloths, mops, eco-friendly solutions
  • Protective gloves, aprons, or masks where required
  • Basic organizational caddies for efficiency

Tips for Building a Reliable Housekeeping Career

  1. Maintain a consistent schedule and respect client privacy.
  2. Seek certifications (hospital sanitation, green cleaning) for higher-paying roles.
  3. Document references and feedback to build trust with new employers.
  4. Invest in ergonomic tools to reduce strain and improve speed.
  5. Stay informed on safety practices and cleaning innovations.

Finding Legitimate Housekeeping Jobs

Trusted Job Boards

  • Indeed, ZipRecruiter, SimplyHired (wide coverage)
  • Care.com (household & nanny-focused)
  • Hospital and hotel career pages

Networking & Direct Outreach

  • Ask satisfied clients for referrals
  • Join local service groups or community boards
  • Leverage LinkedIn to connect with property managers

Red Flags

  • Unclear pay rates or “training fees” upfront
  • No verifiable address or online presence
  • Pressure to sign contracts without reviewing terms

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need certification to become a housekeeper? +

No formal certification is required for most roles, but courses in sanitation or hospitality can improve employability and pay.

What physical requirements should I expect? +

Housekeeping involves standing, bending, and lifting moderate weights for extended periods. Maintaining good posture and stretching reduces fatigue.

Are there opportunities for career growth? +

Yes. With experience, housekeepers often advance to supervisory roles, environmental services management, or private client agencies with higher pay.
